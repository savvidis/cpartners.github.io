Creating your masterpiece

There are many ways to develop a professional, polished resume; of course, we all would like to present a masterpiece, one with a bit of “flair” to showcase our creativity and to stand out in the pile. However, getting the basics down for your resume is critical even before the creativity comes into play.


Length

  • One, clean page should work.
  • Exception: depending on your level of experience in the industry, some resumes may run into two pages, especially for c-level executives.

Contact Information

  • Full name
  • Address
  • Phone number – with a simple, appropriate voicemail greeting
  • Email - Your email address should be professional and one that indicates who you are: (e.g., your_name@yahoo.com)
  • A link to an online profile if available

Objective or Professional Profile

  • This area has become subjective; many resumes today may not follow the traditional flow that highlights “Objective” at the top.
  • However, it is helpful for the reader to be able to understand your goals and expectations in a concise opening statement, which can relate to the specific position or how your expertise relates to the job field you are applying for.

Work Experience

  • List your most recent work experience first.
  • Include the name of the company, its location, your title and the duration of each job.
  • List your responsibilities and the specific results you accomplished using action verbs.
    • For example, "Supervised call center that helped increase customer satisfaction rating by 50% over prior years."
  • The general rule of thumb is to include positions from the last 10 years unless you absolutely believe that earlier work is an exact match to the job you are applying to now.
  • It is important to focus on the previous experience you have that can be directly applied to the position you are seeking, so be sure to edit accordingly.

Skills

  • Computer skills and software programs used. This should include industry specific programs.
  • Certificates
  • Languages spoken
  • It is not necessary to note that you can use Word programs, email or the “Internet.” That is mostly assumed by readers.

Associations and Activities

  • Leadership positions held.
  • Memberships in professional organizations related to the job field.

Other Notable Tips

  • Unnecessary inclusion: "References available upon request." 
    • It is assumed you have references, and further, many organizations are requiring actual reference contacts very early on in the process, so be prepared to have those lined up. 
  • In addition to reference, potential employers will also expect to receive the addendum information required such as work samples, writing samples, background checks, etc., that may become part of the interview process.
  • If there is space left on your resume, you can direct the reader to an online portfolio or your LinkedIn page.
  • Proofread your resume multiple times. Read each word; read it backwards. Have a trusted business contact or friend read it. Read it aloud. Print it out and read it away from the computer. Your resume must be polished; small mistakes may get your resume placed at the bottom of the pile or even in the recycling bin.
