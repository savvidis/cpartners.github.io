All Generations, Apply Within

 

The demographic ground we’re now walking on in the United States is unmapped. Recently, this dynamic has received a lot of press, yet the information presented is often redundant and leaves me thinking, what should companies be doing to optimize this unprecedented level of human capital complexity?

For example, the Baby Boomer generation tends to place high value on group / team interaction, and Generation X, a much smaller generation that had been left to their own devices as they grew up, came into the workplace with an emphasis on independence and work-life balance. This prompted a lot of conflict, with Boomers grumbling about these new employees not being “team players,” and Xers complaining about the incessant “micromanaging.” Of course, now those Xers are managing the Millennials (who grew up in a very different, child-focused, and social-internet-enabled world) and new conflicts emerge, with Millennials expecting more interaction with higher levels of the hierarchy and more substantive work content earlier in their career.

This information can paint a bit of an unnerving picture. Take for example a typical meeting.

Your meeting is about to commence with a room full of employees representing a cross section of generations. Some think a meeting is crucial to performing their job while others think it’s a waste of time. Participants will exchange ideas that some will want to ‘take-away’ to ponder while others will want an immediate answer and resolution. And tension will undoubtedly rise since some will be looking at their smartphones and perceived as not paying attention by those who think it’s rude to break eye contact. And upon leaving the meeting, 40% will want to blog about it, 20% won’t know the first thing about a blog and 30% want a follow-up meeting to discuss the findings of the first meeting.

Meeting minutes may never be the same!

Whether or not they know, or want to admit it, each generation is an essential cog in the greater machine. Accordingly, the successful company is the one that can integrate the contrasting work-styles into the whole [organization] to harness and leverage the best traits of each generation. This then becomes the opportunity that is presented in the talent management landscape. The whole really is greater than the sum of the parts.

So, where do we find such integration? What is the superglue that brings all of these disparate parts together? I believe it is company culture and a dynamic team environment that embraces “all” through a total talent management solution.

Culture of Success

Despite their apparent differences, the commonality shared by all generations is the success of the company.  And that, much like the success of any winning team, is contingent upon the collective contributions of each individual. Keeping everyone actively engaged and stimulated while overseeing daily activities is a juggling act that has to be driven by management. In a recent study titled Engaging the 21st Century Multi-Generational Workforce, the findings suggest that simple and cost-efficient efforts can greatly enhance employee engagement, such as, providing strong training and development opportunities, encouraging work team inclusion, offering customized benefits plans, and promoting a culture of workplace flexibility and supervisor supportiveness.

Without oversight and championing the corporate mantra (through not only communications but actions), it’s easy for ‘divisions’ or ‘groups’ within an organization to fall into silos and adapt their own operating procedures, language or sub-culture. As such, in order to foster a culture of success, management needs to keep the lines of communication open and recognize that what is said is just as important as the medium(s) used to say it. For example, making feedback social is a great example of not just aligning to Millennials’ expectations of the workplace, but also using emerging technology to make corporate culture more engaging for all. By streamlining how employee contributions are recognized as well as the subsequent impact on larger corporate initiatives, the workforce will be less likely to focus on their differences and instead align based on their commonalities and empower the organization to move in one direction: forward!

Dynamic Team

Embracing traditional and contingent labor is an effective and efficient way to ride out common business ‘ebbs and flows’. Supplementing a ‘full-time’ workforce with contractors can address a myriad of needs — from short-term project execution to supplementing in-house resources with specialized talent — all while mitigating unnecessary ramp up costs and additional expenses from resource fluctuations. More importantly, with strong cultural values in place, contingent labor can more quickly become acclimated to not only the task at hand but to operating within the company culture as well. With everyone “beating to the same drum,” workforce integration and management will be more seamless, efficient and flexible.

Total Talent Management

Recognizing this need for assimilating diverse generations into the workplace, we have become a strategic RPO and MSP partner for companies across various industries. Managing and effectively communicating — both internally and externally — across generations requires an individualized approach based on a company’s culture supported by a blend of technology and the ‘human element’. This blended approach has defined our approach to talent management.
While behavioral and historical statistics can paint an interesting picture on the habits and characteristics of these generations, it’s our own culture and people that distinguish Pontoon’s total talent management solutions. There is a lot of data out there but we have more of it – and the means to turn it into future-proof, actionable insight.

So, if you are finding that you have high turnover, recruited resources that don’t meet your needs/with the wrong skillsets, aren’t getting interest from top talent or feel your brand is falling on deaf ears, it may be time for a new tactic. With Pontoon, you can have your voice heard with a message that will speak to all generations.

Isaac Asimov once said, “The only constant is change, continuing change, inevitable change and that is the dominant factor in society today. No sensible decision can be made any longer without taking into account not only the world as it is, but the world as it will be.”

After all, being seen and not heard… is no longer an option.
