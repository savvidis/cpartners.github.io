Market Perspective, April 2013

  • Market Perspective
  • Advisory Board
It's time to change the music

Twenty-two percent (22%) of Americans think now is a good time to find a quality job, nearly the highest measurement since 2008 [Gallup, March]. Seventy-four percent (74%) say now is a “bad time” to find one, reflecting a steady decline from 90% in March 2009. However, today's "bad time" perceptions remain much worse than the pre-recession 49% who thought quality jobs were hard to find in March 2007. While we can look at the numbers and conclude that there is some improvement, it is also apparent that the sentiment of workers continues to be a negative one.

So what about employers?

According to a new CareerBuilder survey, 38 percent of U.S. companies say they have experienced negative implications from extended job vacancies, citing less effective business performance, lower quality work, lower morale and higher employee turnover. When asked about specific losses, 41 percent of those companies have noticed decreased productivity, 21 percent have experienced lost revenue and 22 percent say it is hindering their business’ growth efforts.

This may leave some scratching their heads.

Let’s go back to the principles of supply and demand… If there is a demand for new jobs, and there is a supply of job openings, what is happening here? Is anyone else having a flashback to a junior high dance—the boys on one side of the gym and the girls on the other? And they waited for the music…And while there may be a myriad of factors that are contributing to this apparent disconnect between employers and prospective employees. There appears to be a common denominator. Perception.

We think this presents an incredible opportunity for companies to fill their dance cards, and to fill them with extremely qualified candidates. But, in order to do this, someone needs to start the music and take the first step. Companies can, and should, begin the dance that can interface the demand for jobs with the supply of positions. They need a brand and message that resonates with prospective employees. They need a sustainable strategy for engaging existing internal talent while attracting external talent. They need an agile staffing solution. They need data-driven solutions to bridge the perceived ‘gap’.

Now more than ever, systematic workforce planning will be crucial to quickly source, recruit, and hire top performers. But as businesses continue to move faster and create flexible teams that they can adapt as dynamics change, their success need not be compromised by a “need for speed”.  Process analysis will be essential to hiring and managing their resources—to gauge the effectiveness of factors like team structure, individual progress, collaboration tools, workflows and decision-making processes, and working relationships between team members. Examining data on team interactions and outcomes will help teams keep moving in the right direction, even while sprinting.

Talent acquisition is one of the most dynamic areas of talent management and the technology space is continuing to evolve to meet both the internal and external pressures of today’s economy. At Pontoon, everything we do is data-driven, delivered via award-winning, cutting-edge methodologies—from the retention of existing employees through the screening and on-boarding of new hires. Our flexible “future-proof” solutions allow for scalable, creative solutions that help navigate constraints and maintain compliance. We assist organizations in scrutinizing their workforce plans and designing them to address the lingering economic uncertainty and increased complexity of the workforce.

As technology evolves and economic seasons change, Pontoon will continue to lead through innovative thinking, providing access to the best talent in the industry and transforming the old way of deciphering data into reliable business management tools. War or no war, the numbers tell a compelling story—there is new music playing. No matter how the talent war unfolds, a lack of creativity and vision in talent acquisition will leave some without a dance partner.

Don’t be left at the dance alone.
