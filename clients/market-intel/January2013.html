Keeping it simple

This focus on “human capital” and the notion that “The basic resource in any company is its people” as introduced by Gary Becker, a leading American economist, coupled with current economic uncertainty, employee sentiment and the prevailing skills gap, may renew the war for talent. There has never been a more compelling case to getting talent management right. And by the same token, companies are going to have to take a more strategic and supportive approach to recruiting and retention if they want to find and keep the new breed of evolving talent.

I am reminded of Ockham's razor: the idea that, in trying to understand something, getting unnecessary information out of the way is the fastest way to the truth or to the best explanation. However, it seems the prevailing trend is that it’s easier to complicate than to simplify. So how can we help simplify the talent management process?

To a large extent, Pontoon has done this. The merger of Adecco Solutions and Beeline MSP offers clients a full service solution around total talent management. Our corporate mantra by which we operate and develop our solutions is based on the idea of simplifying the complex, establishing and streamlining the deliverables process. We have done the hard work of capturing and collating data from every step of the talent acquisition lifecycle and have sliced and diced it to create real-time reports and 360 degree feedback to aid not only our own internal processes, but those of our clients.

We recognize that the need for local relevance is demanding greater decentralization. That leading companies have to adopt a more balanced approach whereby local autonomy is combined with globally consistent strategic direction, a shared corporate culture and a unifying set of values. Pontoon can deliver this to our clients as we can draw upon capabilities and resources from nearly anywhere in the world.

So here we wait. Wait to see how things unfold this year, in an air of uncertainty, pounded by a flurry of information from the media that has become so familiar. However, I can’t help but feel that there is something special about 2013 and that it will be a pivotal year. Talent will occupy center stage. Versatility will be the key competency of the future to not only respond to this new world –- but to be positioned to lead it. I feel good about where we’re positioned as a company and feel strongly that we’re poised to be a strategic partner for our clients —- to help them cut through the noise, make the best decisions and focus on their core business. To quote Apple founder Steve Jobs, who didn’t always get it right, but recognized when he had to right the ship, “Deciding what not to do is as important as deciding what to do.”

So, I ask you, does your company’s talent acquisition and management strategy pass the Ockham razor ‘test’?

What is your perspective?
